About the Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal, built between 1632 and 1653, is an iconic white marble mausoleum in Agra, India. It was constructed by the Mughal emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his beloved wife Mumtaz Mahal, who died during childbirth.

The Taj Mahal is known for its stunning architecture, symmetrical gardens, and intricate marble inlay work. It is a UNESCO World Heritage site and one of the New Seven Wonders of the World.
